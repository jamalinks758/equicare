Education

What Every Horse Owner
Should Know

Knowledge is your horse's first line of defence. Here are the fundamentals — when these are right, everything else follows.

01
When to Deworm

Deworm every 4–6 weeks and ensure manure is removed frequently. Rotate products to prevent resistance. Tritack 3-in-1 covers all lifecycle stages.

02
Signs Your Horse Needs Treatment

Watch for: colic, severe lameness, fever, appetite loss, unexplained weight loss, or skin problems during grooming. These are red flags — contact your vet immediately.

03
Daily Hoof Care

Remove stones and mud daily to prevent infection. Schedule farrier trims every 6–8 weeks. Proper steel shoeing provides even weight distribution and protection.

04
Hydration Is Non-Negotiable

Always provide clean, fresh water and shade as Caribbean temperatures rise. Dehydration affects performance and organ function rapidly — prevention is far easier.

05
Stick to a Vaccination Schedule

Consult your vet about core vaccines — tetanus and equine influenza are non-negotiable. Prevention is always cheaper and safer than treating disease.

06
Nutrition & Energy Support

Quality feed should form the majority of your horse's diet. Add minerals when possible and consider Wheat Germ Oil for coat health, stamina, and sustained energy.
